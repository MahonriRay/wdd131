How To Play Magic: The Gathering Commander Format

There are many Magic: The Gathering formats that can be played, Standard, Pauper, etc. One of the most popular formats is Commander, which I will explain how play on this page.

The Commander format of Magic: The Gathering, also known as EDH (Elder Dragon Highlander), is a popular multiplayer variant known for its unique deck-building rules and dynamic gameplay. In this format, each player constructs a 100-card deck led by a legendary creature known as the "Commander," with no more than one copy of any card, except for basic lands. The format emphasizes strategic diversity and social interaction, making it a favorite among casual and competitive players alike. With its emphasis on creativity and personalized playstyles, Commander offers a rich and engaging experience that highlights the expansive possibilities within Magic: The Gathering.

Anatomy of a Magic Card

A Magic: The Gathering card is composed of several key elements that convey its identity and functionality. At the top of the card is the name, followed by the mana cost, which indicates the resources required to play it. Below the name, on the left side, is the card type (such as creature, sorcery, or enchantment) and any relevant subtypes. The main artwork occupies a large central portion, visually representing the card's theme or character. Below the artwork is the text box, which contains the card's abilities, effects, and flavor text that adds thematic depth. Finally, at the bottom, you'll find the card's power and toughness (for creatures), set symbol, collector number, and the artist's credit. These elements work together to provide all the necessary information for using the card in gameplay.

Untap Phase

The Untap Phase is the first step in a player's turn in Magic: The Gathering. During this phase, the player untaps all their tapped cards, making them available for use again. This includes lands, creatures, artifacts, and any other permanents that were tapped during previous turns. The Untap Phase is crucial because it resets the player's resources, allowing them to use their lands for mana and their creatures for attacking or activating abilities in the upcoming phases of their turn. This phase happens automatically and does not allow any actions or responses from either player.

Upkeep Phase

The Upkeep Phase is the second step in a player's turn in Magic: The Gathering, occurring immediately after the Untap Phase. During the Upkeep Phase, any abilities that trigger at the beginning of the upkeep are put onto the stack and can be responded to by either player. This phase is often important for managing ongoing effects, such as paying cumulative upkeep costs or resolving certain triggered abilities. The Upkeep Phase sets the stage for the Draw Phase, ensuring that any necessary upkeep-related actions are addressed before the player draws a card.

Precombat Main Phase

The Precombat Main Phase is a crucial step in a player's turn in Magic: The Gathering, occurring immediately after the Draw Phase and before the Combat Phase. During this phase, players can play lands, cast spells, and activate abilities. It's the primary opportunity for players to develop their board state by summoning creatures, setting up enchantments, and preparing for combat. Decisions made in this phase, such as which creatures to play or what spells to cast, can significantly influence the upcoming combat and the overall strategy for the turn. The Precombat Main Phase allows for a wide range of actions, making it a pivotal point for planning and executing game strategies.

Combat Phase

The Combat Phase is a key part of a player's turn in Magic: The Gathering, where players can attack their opponents and engage in battles with their creatures. This phase is divided into five steps: Beginning of Combat, Declare Attackers, Declare Blockers, Combat Damage, and End of Combat. During the Declare Attackers step, the attacking player chooses which creatures will attack and who or what they will attack. In the Declare Blockers step, the defending player chooses which creatures, if any, will block the attackers. The Combat Damage step involves calculating and assigning damage from the attacking and blocking creatures. This phase is pivotal for applying pressure on opponents, reducing their life total, and managing board presence. Tactical decisions made during the Combat Phase can significantly influence the game's outcome.

Postcombat Main Phase

The Postcombat Main Phase follows the Combat Phase in Magic: The Gathering and serves as a second opportunity for players to cast spells, play lands, and activate abilities. Similar to the Precombat Main Phase, players can use this phase to further develop their board state, respond to changes that occurred during combat, or set up defenses for their opponent's turn. This phase allows for strategic adjustments based on the outcomes of combat, such as summoning new creatures, casting spells that might alter the game state, or playing an additional land if one hasn't been played yet this turn. The Postcombat Main Phase is essential for maximizing a player's tactical options and ensuring they are well-prepared for the next stages of the game.

End Step

The End Step, also known as the End Phase, is the final phase of a player's turn in Magic: The Gathering. During this phase, any abilities that trigger at the "beginning of the end step" are placed on the stack and resolved. This is the time for final actions and responses before the turn passes to the next player. The End Step allows players to cast instant spells and activate abilities one last time before the cleanup step, ensuring they can make final strategic moves or respond to their opponent's actions. It's a crucial moment for setting up defenses or executing plans that need to be in place before the opponent's turn begins.
